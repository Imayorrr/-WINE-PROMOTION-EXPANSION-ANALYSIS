# WINE PROMOTION EXPANSION ANALYSIS
 This report analyzes regional alcohol consumption patterns across Russia to support the expansion of a successful wine promotion campaign originally conducted in Saint Petersburg.

# Dataset used
https://github.com/Imayorrr/-WINE-PROMOTION-EXPANSION-ANALYSIS/blob/main/russian_alcohol_consumption.xls

## Objectives
The objective of this analysis is to determine the top 10 regions that exhibit consumption patterns closely aligned with Saint Petersburg, particularly in terms of wine sales performance, contribution to total alcohol consumption, and overall purchasing behavior.

## Data cleaning process
1. I changed all the column headers to proper format

2. The dataset contains 1616 rows and 7 columns

3. I checked for duplicates but there were no duplicates

4. I checked for blank cells and there were blank cells. I did not delete the blank cells, I calculated the average and I replaced the blank cells with the average

5. The “year” and  “region” columns had no blank cells

# Analysis and insights

## KPI CARDS (Top Metrics)
•	Total Alcohol Consumption (109.99K): Overall consumption is high, showing a strong market.

•	Beer (79.91K): Dominates consumption → most popular category. 

•	Vodka (18.43K): Second highest → still culturally significant. 

•	Wine (8.77K): Relatively low → opportunity for growth. 

•	Brandy & Champagne: Very small share → niche products. 

## Insights
 Wine is underpenetrated, making promotions valuable.

 # 2.  Overall Alcohol Consumption (Pie Chart)
 •	Beer accounts for 73% of total consumption. 
 
•	Vodka contributes 17% 

•	Wine, brandy, and champagne make up a small fraction. 

# 3.  Top 10 Regions for Total Alcohol Consumption
•	Regions like Yamalo-Nenets, Tyumen, and Vologda lead in overall consumption. 

•	These are high-volume markets. 

## Insights
High consumption is not equal to high wine interest.

These regions are good for scale, but not necessarily for wine campaigns.

# 4. 10 Regions Recommended for Wine Promotions
•	Vologda Oblast, Yaroslavl, Zabaykalsky Krai rank highest in average wine consumption per capita. 

•	These regions show stronger wine preference behavior. 

# 5. Yearly Wine Consumption 
•	Wine consumption steadily increased over time. 

•	Peak around 2009–2011, followed by slight fluctuations. 

•	Overall trend remains upward. 

## Insights
Wine demand is growing, supporting expansion of promotions.

# OVERALL CONCLUSION
•	The market is dominated by beers

•	Wine is growing and underutilized 

•	Targeting regions with higher wine consumption patterns (not just total sales) is the smartest strategy
